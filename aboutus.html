About Us

The low-down on AWC

 

Who Are We?

The Association for Women in Computing is a group of diverse students who all have a goal in mind - to be a part of a supportive, positive community and to support gender diversity in computer science.

 

What Do We Do?

We host all sorts of events: homework nights, guests speakers on topics of interest to our members, volunteer within our community, and so much more! 

 

When and Where Do We Meet?

We meet every other Thursday at 5PM during the fall and spring semesters in Barnard 347 (the CS conference room). We switch off with ACM, so come every Thursday for the full experience of what our computer science department has to offer!

 

Why Are We Here?

Take a seat in one of our classes, or take a look around a software company. There's a severe lack of women in the computer science field. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, only 32% of web developers, 21% of computer programmers, and 19% of software developers are women. It's rough being a minority in a field - AWC aims to provide support to these women so that they may achieve the most!
